    About me

    I am Navid Bin Hasan, currently working as a Lecturer at the Department of Computer Science & Engineering of Bangladesh University of Engineering & Technology. I completed my Bachelors degree from the same department this May, 2022. I am pursuing my Master's degree at the same department. Currently I am aiming for a research oriented career in the field of Compuational Biology and Bioinformatics. As a bioinformatics researcher, I wish to contribute to research that can have far-reaching impacts, such as working on projects related to complex diseases to enhance the associated knowledge and treatment, uncovering evolutionary mysteries of different ecologies, etc. I also have a great interest in evolutionary and population genetics. Moreover, I have always found interest in theoretical computer science, data structures, and algorithms.

    During my bachelors degree,I worked in Bioinformatics, especially Phylogeny, under the supervision of Dr. Md. Shamsuzzoha Bayzid as my thesis. We worked in collaboration with Dr. Siavash Mirarab from the department of EECS, UCSD. We incorporated branch support estimation to distance based phylogenetic placement methods. We devised a superfast linear algebraic implementation of non-parametric bootstrapping, called Fast-Bootstrapping for estimating support values in APPLES. During my MSc, under Prof. Bayzid, I have worked extensively on quartet-based coalescent species tree estimation methods. We performed a comprehensive comparative study on different weighted quartet distribution generation techniques. We also developed a new heuristic algorithm, QT-WEAVER, for "correcting" weighted quartet disttributions which outperforms top methods like ASTRAL, wQFM convincingly.

    Research Experience

    1. Leveraging and Correcting Weighted Quartet Distributions for Enhanced Species Tree Inference

      April 2022 - Present

      Bioinformatics

      Species Tree Estimation

      Summary Methods

      Quartet Amalgamation

      A comparative analysis of several weighted quartet distribution estimation techniques and their effects on the downstream species tree estimation under the supervision of Dr. Md. Shamsuzzoha Bayzid. This study has provided a comprehensive insight into the relative performance of a compendium of weighted quartet generation techniques. The manuscript is under review in Genome Biology and Evolution [biorxiv].


      We have also been working on a novel algorithm which can correct weighted quartet distributions for Gene Tree Estimation Errors (GTEE) without resorting to any reference trees. Our heuristic algorithm finds the amount of "conflict" each quartet topology is under and tries to balance the weights based on its findings. The tool is available on github: QT-WEAVER. Our work is currently under reveiw in RECOMB 2025.

    2. Branch Support Estimation in Distance-Based Phylogenetic Placement Methods

      March 2021 - April 2022

      Bioinformatics

      Phylogenetic Placement

      Branch Support

      Bootstrapping

      I worked on this project under Dr. Md. Shamsuzzoha Bayzid and Dr. Siavash Mirarab. Here, we build on the recently developed distance-based phylogenetic placement methodology and show how the distribution of placements can be estimated per input sequence. We compare parametric and non-parametric sampling methods, showing that non-parametric bootstrapping is far more accurate in estimating uncertainty. Finally, we design and implement a linear algebraic implementation of bootstrapping that makes it faster, and we incorporate the computation of support values as a new feature in the APPLES software. Our work was published on RECOMB-CG and Biology.

    3. Research Assistant, Samsung Research Bangladesh-CSE, BUET Collaboration Project

      July 2023 - January 2024

      Machine Learning

      LLM

      Android Testing

      Prompt Engineering

      I worked as a part-time research assistant in a collaborative research project between Samsung Research BD and CSE, BUET under the supervision of Dr. Anindya Iqbal. The project was aimed at generating effective test cases for Android apps and executing them; both completely automatically. We designed a UI explorer to exhaustively navigate an Android application and create an accurate menu tree. We designed a DFS-inspired search algorithm tailored to tackle the many challenges of navigating an Android app. We then developed a recursive test case generator from the menu tree. The execution tool is designed to automatically execute test cases and observe the outcomes. It can also verify whether the actions satisfy the expected outcomes. For this, we first collected and curated a diverse dataset and fine-tuned several open-source models such as Llama. With advanced prompt engineering, the fine-tuned execution model showed promising results. Our seven-month work impressed the Samsung Research HQ, and the project was extended for one more year.

    4. Development of LLM Based Test Case Generator from Use Cases

      January 2024 - Present

      Machine Learning

      LLM

      Fine-Tuning

      In this study, we aim to enhance the practice of early test case generation using design documents such as use cases under the supervision of Dr. Anindya Iqbal. We collected and carefully curated a comprehensive use case to test cases dataset with help from undergraduate students, industry experts, faculties. We then leveraged this dataset to fine-tune open-source models like Llama 3.1 8B and Mistral 7B which enhances their ability to generate high quality test cases. Our work is currently under review in MSR 2025.
